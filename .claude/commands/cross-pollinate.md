You are operating against the cosmic-horror-atlas corpus. Read `CLAUDE.md` first if you have not in this session.

## Task

Find non-obvious connections between: **$ARGUMENTS**

The two arguments may be authors, concepts, techniques, or principles. Parse accordingly.

Steps:

1. Locate the relevant index/entry files for both inputs.
2. Read each fully, plus their **Related** chains one level out.
3. Generate connections in three tiers:

   - **Surface** — connections that are obvious or already noted in either entry's Related field. Include briefly so the user knows you saw them.
   - **Structural** — connections at the level of craft mechanics: shared techniques deployed for different effects, opposite techniques deployed for similar effects, mirrored failure modes.
   - **Deep / non-obvious** — connections that emerge only when both bodies of work are held in mind together. These are the point of the command. Aim for 2-3 of these.

4. For each non-obvious connection, end with: "If this holds, the corpus should have an entry on X" — proposing a new concept or principle that would capture the connection.

Constraints:

- The "non-obvious" tier is the deliverable. Do not pad it with restated surface connections.
- If after honest analysis there really are no non-obvious connections, say so. False insight is worse than no insight.
- High effort.
