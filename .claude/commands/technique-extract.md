You are operating against the cosmic-horror-atlas corpus. Read `CLAUDE.md` first if you have not in this session.

## Task

Identify the underlying craft move in: **$ARGUMENTS**

The argument may be a short quoted passage, a description of a scene, a structural pattern from a story, or a paraphrased moment. Parse accordingly.

Steps:

1. Restate what is happening in the passage/scene at the level of mechanics, not plot. ("A character is told something but the telling is interrupted before the key noun" — not "Lovecraft's narrator finds out about Cthulhu.")
2. Name the craft move(s) at work. Use existing terminology from the atlas (`techniques/`) when possible.
3. For each move, search the corpus for matching `techniques/` and `concepts/` entries. Link them.
4. If the move is not yet in the atlas, draft a stub:

   - Proposed title (kebab-case slug + display title)
   - One-sentence definition
   - Two or three sources (within the atlas's covered authors) where the same move appears
   - Suggested tags
   - Whether this should land in `techniques/`, `concepts/`, or `principles/`

5. Offer to write the full entry on confirmation. Do not write it unprompted.

Constraints:

- The deliverable is a *named, transferable* craft move — something a writer could deploy in a new context. "Lovecraft is creepy here" fails. "Withholding the noun in an apparent moment of revelation" passes.
- High effort.
