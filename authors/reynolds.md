# Alastair Reynolds

**Active:** 1990–present
**Tradition:** British hard SF; the *Revelation Space* sequence as the working example

## Why he's in the atlas

Reynolds is a clean test case for [inherit-structure-not-surface](../principles/inherit-structure-not-surface.md). The *Revelation Space* universe inherits Lovecraft's structural commitments — deep time, hostile cosmos, civilizations annihilated by something that does not negotiate, knowledge as the failure mode — and dresses none of it in Lovecraftian surface signatures. There are no eldritch adjectives. There is no Necronomicon. The Inhibitors are mechanical, plausible, and terrifying *because* they are explained in physical terms rather than mystical ones.

Worth studying:
- Hard-SF rigor used to amplify rather than dispel cosmic dread
- Deep time as a structural element — Reynolds operates on million-year arcs without losing weight
- The Inhibitors as a Great Filter rendered as horror, not as physics speculation
- "Cosmic indifference" instantiated as something physically explicable, which is harder and arguably scarier than instantiating it as something occult
- In-copyright — paraphrase and analyze, quote sparingly
