# Clark Ashton Smith

**Active:** 1910s–1960s
**Tradition:** American weird; the third pillar of *Weird Tales* alongside Lovecraft and Howard. Sword-and-sorcery, dying-earth, and decadent fantasy registers.

## Why he's in the atlas

Smith is the prose stylist of the *Weird Tales* generation — the one whose surface execution is genuinely worth studying, where Lovecraft's mostly is not. His dying-earth cycles (Zothique, Hyperborea, Averoigne, Poseidonis) construct cosmologies that are *aesthetically* coherent without being *systematically* coherent: a useful midpoint between Lovecraft's deliberate incoherence and Derleth's overtidied pantheon.

Worth studying:
- Decadent prose as a tool, not as decoration — the lushness does work
- Dying-earth as a frame that bakes cosmic indifference into the setting without needing to argue for it
- Cosmologies built by aesthetic resonance rather than by system
- Public domain — quote freely

The standard caveat: his prose register is hard to inherit without parody. Easier to study what it does than to imitate what it sounds like.
