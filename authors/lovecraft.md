# H.P. Lovecraft

**Active:** 1917–1937
**Tradition:** American weird fiction. The structural founder of cosmic horror as a genre.

## Why he's in the atlas

Lovecraft is unavoidable but not uncritically inheritable. The atlas treats him as a *case study in selective inheritance*: a writer whose structural innovations are still generative a century later, and whose surface execution should mostly be left in the early twentieth century where it belongs.

Worth inheriting:
- Cosmic indifference as a structural commitment, not a thematic flourish
- Refusal to systematize the mythos — see [unreliable-cosmology](../techniques/unreliable-cosmology.md)
- Atmosphere as primary load-bearing element

Not worth inheriting:
- The prose
- The characters
- The racism, which is not extricable by editing a few names — it leaks into the cosmology itself in ways the work has to be read against, not edited around

The general principle his case demonstrates — separate structure from surface when inheriting from any tradition — is captured in [inherit-structure-not-surface](../principles/inherit-structure-not-surface.md).
