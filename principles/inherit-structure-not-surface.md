# Inherit Structure, Not Surface

**Type:** principle
**Sources:** H.P. Lovecraft (proof case); Alastair Reynolds, Stanislaw Lem (downstream inheritors)
**Tags:** inheritance, mythos, cosmic-indifference, lovecraft
**Related:** [unreliable-cosmology](../techniques/unreliable-cosmology.md), [lovecraft](../authors/lovecraft.md)

## Summary

When inheriting from a tradition, separate the structural innovations from the surface execution. Take what is load-bearing; leave what is dated, embarrassing, or actively bad. Lovecraft is the archetypal case: his structural commitments are still generative a century later, while his prose, his characters, and his politics are mostly liabilities.

## Analysis

A common failure mode in genre work is inheriting a master's surface signatures as if they were the source of the power. Lovecraft pastiche is the textbook example — adjective stacks, narrators undone by single sentences, italicized last lines, the same handful of place names. The result reads as costume, because what made Lovecraft work was never the prose.

What was actually load-bearing in Lovecraft, and is worth inheriting:

- **Cosmic indifference as a structural commitment, not a thematic gesture.** Humanity is genuinely not central to the story's frame. Knowledge is not a path to safety; it is the failure mode. The protagonist's investigative competence does not earn them survival.
- **Refusal to systematize the mythos.** Lovecraft did not have a coherent pantheon. Different stories give different accounts of the same entity. Yog-Sothoth is not in a fixed relationship to Azathoth. The contradictions are the point — see [unreliable-cosmology](../techniques/unreliable-cosmology.md). The Derleth elementalization, which assigned each Great Old One a Greek-style affinity and arranged them into hierarchies, is a downgrade dressed as a finishing touch.
- **Atmosphere as primary load-bearing element**, with plot subordinated to it.

What is not worth inheriting:

- The prose. Purple, repetitive, fond of words like "eldritch" and "non-Euclidean" used as if they did the imaginative work themselves.
- The characters. Walking nervous breakdowns, narrators who exist primarily to be appalled.
- The racism, which leaks into the cosmology itself in ways that aren't extricable by editing a few names.

The principle generalizes beyond Lovecraft. Tolkien's structural model — deep invisible scaffolding under shallow visible story (see [invisible-scaffolding](../techniques/invisible-scaffolding.md)) — is more transferable than his prose register, which only works in his own voice. Ligotti's syntactic patterns are inseparable from Ligotti and cannot be copied without parody. His philosophical commitments can.

The diagnostic question when inheriting: *if I removed this surface signature, would the work still do what I want it to do?* If yes, the signature is not load-bearing and you do not need it. If no, you have identified what to study.

The corollary: the writers who most visibly imitate a master usually understand them least. The writers who carry forward what actually mattered tend to look, on the surface, nothing like the source.
